Ticket Debt in Chicago's 6th Ward

You can fight your ticket in court and win!

Although, in the 6th ward, only 10% of tickets get contested, those which do win their in court cases 65% of the time.

Important Deadlines

For a parking or compliance violation, you have 7 days from the date your ticket was issued to contest it. If a contest or payment has not been made by then, you will be given another 21 days to request a hearing.

For traffic law violations caught by an automated camera (red light or speeding violations) you are given 21 days from the Notice of Violation to contest the ticket or a Notice of Determination will be mailed to you detailing that you have been found liable.

However, you still have the option to petition to stop the default judgement by appearing in person within 21 days after you have received the Notice of Determination. After this you can no longer contest the ticket.

After 25 days of the Notice of Determination you will be mailed a Notice of Final Determination detailing that you can no longer contest the ticket and a penalty equal to the amount of the fine will be given.

How to win your case in court

For parking or compliance violations, including the most common violations in the 6th ward (no city sticker, no standing/parking, street cleaning, expired plates, missing plates), the following defenses could be used:

  • You were not the owner of or were not leasing the vehicle at the time the ticket was issued
  • The vehicle or its plates were stolen when the ticket was issued
  • The signs prohibiting or restricting parking were missing or were too difficult to see
  • The parking meter was not functional and not because of anything you did
  • The alleged violations are not consistent with what was brought up during the time the ticket was issued (e.g., You may contest a city sticker violation if you have resided in Chicago for less than 30 days or you purchased the cited vehicle in the last 30 days, tinted windows can be a required medical accessory when complying with 5 ILCS 5/12-503(g); the residential parking permit was issued to a delivery, service, or repair vehicle or home health care provider doing business with or assisting a resident of the zone; etc.);
  • The illegal condition cited did not exist at the time the ticket was issued
  • The compliance violation has been corrected prior to the judge making a ruling; however, you must ensure you are not applicable to other compliance violations at this time
